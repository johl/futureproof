W hen the internet was emerging roughly 20 years ago, many interesting speculations were made about how it would change our lives. The internet promised to completely replace the need for newspapers! And television! And we'd all have to speak Esperanto! It was to be a brave new world of equality where people could truly be judged on the content of their character, and the whole world would be constantly, and instantaneously, connected.

Some ten years later, during the original "dot-com" boom, companies rushed headlong at this vision and wrote dream-like business plans that vied to replace every part of our lives with an online alternative. But then the bubble burst, and a lot of people stopped believing that the internet would have the deep impact on our lives that were originally promised. Clifford Stoll of Newsweek wrote "Do our computer pundits lack all common sense? The truth is no online database will replace your daily newspaper, no CD-ROM can take the place of a competent teacher, and no computer network will change the way government works." Now, only 15 years later, we've begun to prove each of these claims false.

Newspaper and publishing giants are folding, along with terrestrial television and radio. Sites like amazon.com have fundamentally changed retail. We've seen the large-scale obsolescence of everyday objects, and the emergence of a plethora of new tools and gadgets. Entire professions are disappearing and new ones are surfacing. Perhaps the biggest change we've seen so far is in how we conduct our social and political lives. Massive relief efforts for earthquakes and floods have been organized. Corrupt politicians outed on Twitter, and underdog candidates pushed to the forefront through micro-financing efforts. We're even starting to see whole regimes overthrown due to online organization. Ultimately, the 20-year-old dreams of science fiction, the speculations that made us rejoice, recoil, or regroup, are becoming real.

So…the question we're asking with this site is: "What will the next 20 years be like?" Take a look around your life and ask yourself how much if it will still be the same? Will your job exist? The things in your house? The way you get things done? Will your kids learn the same thing in school that you did?

Have fun with it! Think of it like "Hot or Not" for the future. We hope some will make you think and others will make you laugh. Once you’ve voted on a few predictions, add some of you own, or share it with your friends. And remember… the best way to predict the future is to create it!
